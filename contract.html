Group Contract

1.Team Meeting Ground Rules

1.1 Time and frequency: Each meeting should be recorded and at least lasts 25min, and the frequency should be guaranteed at least once a week.
1.2 Attendance: Each group member should attend weekly meetings if there is no special reason. If group member cannot attend, notifications should be posted on teams in advance.
1.3 Preparation: After each weekâ€™s tutorial, all members should contribute their ideas on the topic of weekly meetings.
1.4 Keep issues and answer that arise in meetings in confidence within the team unless otherwise indicated
1.5 Be honest and open during meetings
1.6 Encourage a diversity of opinions on all topics.
1.7 Attend the meeting on time and promise not to quit early.
1.8 Group should communicate and have meetings only in Teams. Members should not communicate on other social media.
1.9 While meeting, group members should have full concentration on discussing. Doing anything else other than that is not allowed.

2.Task Allocation Policy

2.1 Each group member will work to the best of his / her ability - making sure to complete the tasks assigned on the workload table, ensure that it is up to standards, and it is completed with punctuality.
2.2 Each team member must complete his/her task before the deadline and reflect it to Trello in real time during the task.
2.3 If changes are made to certain processes in a task, they should also be reflected in Trello.
2.4 Individual group members should be listed with specific jobs related to the final project so that all know who is responsible for each task.

3.Unmet Problem Policy

3.1 Ensure that problems can be identified in advance and solved in groups.
3.2 When unmet problem arises and group members cannot solve that by themselves. Group member should post the specific problem on the MS teams so that every member could see and contribute ideas on the problem. If the problem is difficult, team should seek help from mentor as soon as possible.

4.Agree-upon Policy

4.1 Give everyone the opportunity for equal participation.
4.2 Be open to new approaches and listen to new ideas.
4.3 Avoid placing blame when things go wrong. Instead, we will discuss the process and explore.
4.4 Teammates should be honest about their process, if any problem occurs, each teammate has the responsibility of informing other group members.

5.Response Frequency

5.1 Within group members, response frequency should be guaranteed within 24 hours.
5.2 In case of special circumstances, the team members should be informed in advance to increase the response frequency.
5.3 Regard to the mentor, it is expected to receive a response to within 48hours.

6.Tool Use Policy

6.1 Team members should ensure that the same coding tools are used and that the tools are installed, and the system environment is configured before the task begins.
6.2 Team members should use the same IDE, in this case, we are using IntelliJ.
6.3 All codes should be shared on the Github and upload to the bucket on the AWS.
